01 · Introduction

Meet Gary, your AI
Chief of Staff

Gary lives on your Mac and runs quietly in the background — managing your calendar, triaging your inbox, tracking tasks, and dispatching complex work to specialized AI agents. It's your personal mission control.

Unlike a chatbot you visit, Gary is an ambient intelligence layer. It syncs your Google Calendar and Gmail, keeps your task list (called "Open Loops") organized using GTD principles, and lets you delegate anything from drafting an email to writing code — all from a unified interface.

📅
Calendar & Email
7-day calendar view, Gmail sync, smart email drafting and send — all connected via Google OAuth.
🔁
Open Loops
A GTD-style task system with urgency levels, contexts, owners, and due dates. Never lose track of a commitment.
📝
Notes & Memory
Capture ideas, meeting notes, and living docs. Full-text search, tags, and AI panel for instant summarization.
🤖
Agent Dispatch
Spawn Claude Code for dev tasks, Cowork for research. Gary routes work to the right agent automatically.
🌅
Morning Brief
An AI-generated daily briefing summarizing your calendar, open priorities, and anything that needs your attention.
📱
Telegram Bridge
Capture tasks and notes from your phone via Telegram. Send voice messages — Gary transcribes them automatically.
💡
Philosophy Gary is local-first. All your data lives in a SQLite database on your Mac — no cloud sync, no data sharing. AI calls go directly to Anthropic/OpenAI from your machine.
02 · Setup

Getting Started

Gary is a native macOS app. Setup takes about 5 minutes. The most important step is connecting your Google account — this powers calendar and Gmail integration.

1
Launch Gary
Open Gary from your Applications folder. The main window opens to Mission Control — your central dashboard.
2
Connect Google Account
Open Settings → Google tab. Click "Connect Google Account". A browser window will open for OAuth authorization. Grant access to Calendar and Gmail.

Once connected, Gary immediately syncs your next 7 days of calendar events and your 20 most recent unread emails.
3
Add Your AI API Key
Open Settings → AI tab. Add your Anthropic API key (required for Gary's chat and background intelligence). Optionally add an OpenAI or OpenRouter key for additional models.

Gary routes chat through Claude Haiku by default — fast and cost-effective. Background jobs use Gemini Flash Lite (pennies per day).
4
Review Your Morning Brief
On your first full day, Gary will generate a Morning Brief — an AI summary of what's on your plate. It pulls from your calendar, recent emails, and open tasks. Regenerate it anytime from the dashboard.
5
Explore the Command Palette
Press ⌘K to open the command palette. This is Gary's primary interaction surface — search for actions, launch features, and give Gary instructions without navigating menus.
⚠
Google Permissions Gary requests read/write access to Gmail (for drafting + sending) and read access to Calendar. Tokens are stored locally in your SQLite database — never on any server.

04 · Task Management

Creating Open Loops

Open Loops are Gary's task system, built on GTD (Getting Things Done) principles. A "loop" represents any open commitment — something you said you'd do, something you're waiting on, or anything that needs a next action.

Creating a Loop

Three ways to create a loop:

A
Command Palette
Press ⌘K and type "Create a loop to [task] by [date]". Gary extracts the title, due date, and urgency automatically.
B
Quick Capture Hotkey
Press ⌘⇧L to open the loop capture modal anywhere in Gary. Fill in the title and optional metadata.
C
From Email or Calendar
Gary can auto-detect loops from your emails. Look for the "?" flag on emails that mention commitments or action items.

Anatomy of a Loop

Review Q1 metrics deck before board meeting
urgent @review owner: Willie · Due Fri, Mar 7 · Source: email
Write up API docs for internal wiki
normal @writing owner: Willie · No due date · Source: notes
Explore new podcast recording setup options
someday @research · No due date

Loop Fields

FieldOptionsNotes
Urgency urgent normal low someday Drives sorting in the dashboard
Context @calls @review @writing @errands GTD contexts for batching similar tasks
Owner Free text (your name, team name, etc.) Useful for delegated tasks you're tracking
Due Date Any date Loops with past-due dates surface prominently
Source email, calendar, shadow, manual, telegram Auto-populated based on origin
✦
Best Practice Capture everything — even vague ideas. Use someday urgency for things that aren't actionable yet. A weekly review (Sunday morning) is the best time to process and prioritize your loops.
05 · Knowledge Base

Creating Notes

Notes in Gary are more than text files — they're living documents that connect to your tasks, emails, and AI context. Every note is full-text searchable and can be tagged, pinned, and enriched by AI.

Creating a Note

⌨
Press ⌘⇧N → opens quick note capture anywhere in Gary
💬
Tell Gary: "New note: [title or content]"
📋
Use ⌘⇧T to paste a meeting transcript → Gary extracts action items automatically

Tagging System

Tags keep your notes organized without rigid folders. Use them freely — Gary's search is powerful enough that you can always find notes by content.

#meeting #project-x #ideas #research #reference #client-acme #weekly-review #personal

Living Documents

Some notes evolve over time rather than being one-off captures. Use pinned notes for documents you return to frequently:

# Examples of living docs worth keeping pinned

· "Q2 Goals"       — updated weekly with progress
· "Client: Acme"   — all context, contacts, history in one place
· "Ideas Inbox"    — quick-capture dumping ground, process monthly
· "API Reference"  — internal docs, linked from loops when needed
· "Weekly Review"  — rolling log of Sunday reviews
ℹ

06 · Direct Communication

Chat Interface

Gary's chat interface is a full conversational AI assistant with context about your life. Unlike a generic chatbot, Gary knows your calendar, your open tasks, your notes, and your communication patterns — so its responses are grounded in your actual situation.

Opening Chat

Click the AI Assistant app in your taskbar, or type in the compact chat bar at the bottom of Mission Control. For a full conversation view, open the AI Assistant window from the app dock.

Gary AI Assistant
You: What should I focus on today?

Gary: Based on your day:
📅 You have a 1pm Product Review — your "Q1 metrics deck" loop is overdue.
✉️ 3 unread emails need replies, including one from Sarah marked urgent.
🔁 Your highest-urgency loop: "Finalize pricing proposal" · due today.

I'd suggest starting with the pricing proposal — want me to pull up your notes on it?

You: Yes, and draft a reply to Sarah.
Gary: Opening your pricing notes... and pulling Sarah's email. Draft coming up.

Context Window Gary keeps the last 30 messages in your conversation, with the most recent 10 getting full detail. Older messages are compressed to preserve context without hitting token limits. Long conversations stay coherent.

Conversation Management

The conversation sidebar lets you manage multiple ongoing threads. Each conversation can have a title, and Gary remembers context within a session. Start a new conversation when switching to an unrelated topic.

07 · Mobile Bridge

Telegram Integration

Gary has a Telegram bot that extends its capabilities to your phone. Capture tasks on the go, send voice memos that get transcribed, and receive proactive notifications — all without opening your Mac.

Setup

1
Create a Telegram Bot
Open Telegram and message @BotFather. Send /newbot, follow the prompts, and copy the bot token you receive.
2
Configure in Gary Settings
Open Settings → Connectors → Telegram. Paste your bot token and optionally an OpenAI key (for voice transcription via Whisper).
3
Start the Bot
Enable the Telegram integration in Settings. Gary starts a long-polling listener. Open Telegram on your phone, find your bot, and send it a message to test.
4
Enable Voice Transcription (Optional)
Add an OpenAI key in Settings → Connectors → Telegram. Now when you send a voice message to your bot, Gary transcribes it via Whisper and creates a note automatically.

What the Telegram Bot Can Do

🎙
Send a voice message → Gary transcribes via Whisper and saves as a note
📸
Send a photo → Claude Vision analyzes it (business card, whiteboard, etc.)
💬
Send any text → processed as a capture (note, loop, or Gary response)
📎
Send a video → audio extracted, transcribed, and saved
✦
Use Case Walking between meetings, have a thought? Open Telegram, send a quick voice memo. By the time you're back at your desk, Gary has transcribed it and either created a loop or saved a note — depending on what you said.

Morning Brief Optimization

The morning brief is most useful when Gary has rich context to draw from. Improve it by:

✦
Tips for a Better Brief
  • Keep Open Loops up to date — Gary uses them to identify your priorities
  • Process meeting transcripts with ⌘⇧T — Gary learns project context
  • Check in daily using the Daily Check-in modal — your answers inform Gary's tone
  • Pin your most important ongoing notes so Gary can reference them

Transcript Pipeline

After any meeting, paste the transcript into Gary with ⌘⇧T. Gary will:

1
Store the full transcript
Saved in a searchable FTS5 index — ask Gary "what did we decide about X last Tuesday?" and it'll find it.
2
Extract action items
AI scans for commitments, decisions, and next steps — surfaces them as Loop suggestions you can approve or reject.
3
Update your profile context
Key people, projects, and decisions get added to your Willie Profile — context Gary injects into every future AI interaction.

In 2016, Geoffrey Hinton — the "godfather of AI" — predicted that radiologists would become obsolete within 5 years. He was wrong. Not because AI couldn't read medical images (it can, often better than humans), but because radiologists evolved. They became AI-augmented diagnosticians — using machine learning to catch what the human eye misses, spending less time on pattern recognition and more time on complex cases, patient communication, and clinical judgment.

The lesson: AI doesn't replace expertise. It amplifies it.

The same transformation is happening in venue operations, project management, and client intelligence. The question isn't whether AI will change PMY's work — it's whether PMY will lead that change or be disrupted by it.
